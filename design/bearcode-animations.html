BearCode / animation lab v2

Thinking paw
Now 8-bit like the rest of the family: five blocky toe pads, single-pixel claws splaying outward, one chunky palm on a 26×24 grid. Each digit (claw + toe together) lights up in sequence, then the palm — opacity only, so the pixels never shimmer. The 17px one lives in the Working… header.
8-bit grizzly · walk cycle
De-cameled on a 26×13 grid: the head is big and nearly hump-height, the neck is thick and short, the body is deep, the legs are stocky. The long low camel neck is gone. Still blinks.
Thinking grizzly · front-facing roar cycle
Head-on grizzly, deliberately not a teddy: small eyes under a heavy dark brow, wide flat nose, full-width furry cheeks. The thinking animation is the mouth: closed, cracked, full roar with teeth and a dropped jaw, on a slow chomp rhythm. Still blinks between roars. Shown at 9x, 4x, and header size, candidate replacement for the paw in the Working… header.
